Proteins

Proteins are essential macromolecules that play a key role in numerous physiological processes.

Benefits of Protein

  • Muscle Growth and Repair: Protein supports muscle repair and growth after physical activity.
  • Enzyme Production: Catalyzes biochemical reactions essential for life.
  • Immune Function: Supports the immune system with antibodies and cytokines.
  • Hormone Regulation: Helps produce hormones like insulin and growth hormone.
  • Cell Structure: Provides structural support and aids in communication between cells.
  • Tissue Repair: Essential for the repair of skin, organs, and connective tissues.
  • Transport and Storage: Hemoglobin and ferritin are key protein examples.
  • Healthy Skin, Hair, and Nails: Proteins like collagen and keratin provide strength and elasticity.
  • Energy Source: Acts as a secondary source of energy when required.
  • Satiety and Weight Management: Keeps you fuller for longer, aiding in weight control.
  • Bone Health: Collagen supports bone density and strength.

Effects of Protein Deficiency

A lack of protein can lead to severe health issues, including:

  • Muscle Wasting: Leads to weakness and reduced stamina.
  • Weakened Immunity: Increases susceptibility to infections.
  • Edema: Causes swelling due to fluid imbalance.
  • Stunted Growth: Affects children's development and brain function.
  • Hair, Skin, and Nail Issues: Results in brittle nails, hair loss, and skin problems.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Impacts metabolism and growth.
  • Anemia: Reduces red blood cell production, causing fatigue.
  • Delayed Healing: Slows tissue repair and wound healing.
